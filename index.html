TradeMe.co.nz

A guide to using TradeMe

TradeMe.co.nz is a very popular buy and sell site based in New Zealand where you can list items you would like to sell, and browse items you might like to buy. This tutorial will guide you through creating and activating an account, browsing goods listed on trademe, and list an item you might like to sell. This tutorial includes step by step instructions coupled with short videos and quizes and should take about thirty minutes to complete.

Objectives of this tutorial

Learn how to register for trademe

Learn how to navigate the Homepage

Learn how to find specific items for sale

Learn how to make a bid and complete a purchase

Learn how to sell an item

Creating an Account

The objective for this lesson is to be able to register and sign in to TradeMe

Step 1. Open your Web browser and type in www.trademe.co.nz

Step 2. Click on the register button in the top right hand corner

Step 3. Enter your details and hit submit

Step 4. Check your email inbox for an email from trademe, they will send you a verification email

Step 5. Open the email they sent and click on the "yip it's me" button, this will take you to the login page

step 6. Enter your details into the boxes

Step 7. You will be met with this message when you login:

It is highly recommended you become an authenticated member. This will allow you to sell items on trademe and forms part of the stratagy for fraud prevention keeping you and everyone else safe.

Quick exercise

Try creating your own account, you can always become authenticated later on if you need too!

Browsing for items Trademe

A guide to using Search functions to find items. The objective for this lesson is to be able to search for an item on TradeMe.

Please watch the folling video tutorial to find out more about items for sale on Trademe as well as how to narrow your search using the available search functions on Trademe

Other ways to search items using trademe

There are several other ways to find items using trademe search functions. Below we'll look at the marketplace menu.

You'll notice four tabs at the top labelled 'Marketplace', 'Jobs', 'Motors', and 'Property'. These tabs represent the four main catagories you can search under when using trademe. For this tutorial we will only look at the Marketplace which is the default setting.

There are many common catagories in this search tool and each catagory links to more sub catagories much like searching using the search bar tool(see above video).

If you would like to save an item you are interested in to look at later you can add it to your 'Watchlist' by clicking the button shown below in yellow on the item page.

We will look more at the watchlist in the next session called 'Buying'

Quick exercise

Try finding the most expensive teapot on trademe(it's probably lavish). Start by using the search bar and typing in Teapot. hint: narrow your search by selecting kitchen.

Buying an item on Trademe

A guide to buying an item or watching an item on Trademe. The objective of this lesson is to be able to purchase or bid on an item.

When you have found an item you would like to buy there are several ways you can purchase the product. Firstly, it is recommended you add the item to your 'Watchlist'. The 'add to watchlist' button is shown below in yellow.

Your watchlist will hold all items you are interested in or have placed a bid on. You can view your watchlist by clicking on the 'Watchlist' menu item in the yellow menu bar shown below.

When you have found an item you would like to purchase you can either 'Buy Now' if the option is available(not all auctions have a buy now) or place a 'bid' on the item. Once you have bid on an item you are legally required to complete the purchase if you win the auction. Please watch the short video below to learn more about bidding

How to use Auto-bids

Auto-bids keep you in the lead by placing a bid for you whenever you’re outbid. When you’re outbid Trade Me will place the minimum allowable bid until you win the auction or your auto-bid is exceeded.

To place an auto-bid:

1. Enter the most you are prepared to pay in the bid box

2. Tick 'Auto-bid'

3. Click 'Set auto-bid'

4. Confirm your bid

5. When you're done, Trade Me will keep you in the lead until you win or your autobid is exceeded

You can increase an existing auto-bid by placing a higher value auto-bid. No additional bid will be placed if you lead the bidding. You cannot decrease the value of your auto-bid.

Quick exercise

Find an item you would be interested in purchasing and add it to your watchlist - If you really want the item try placing a bid on it!

Selling an item on Trademe

A guide to listing an item you want to sell. The objective of this lesson is to be able to list an item you wish to sell on TradeMe

To illustrate the steps needed to list an item to sell we will list a bottle of LCD screen cleaner for sale for $1.

When you would like to list an item to sell, you will need to click on the sell button and choose 'general item'.

This will take you to the initial 'list an item' screen where you can select which catagory you would like to list the item in. We will choose computers in box 1, Laptops in box 2, then other in box 3

then click 'List in only one catagory' then click the next button.

This will take you to the 'Listing details' page where we can add a listing title, subtitle and description. In the listing title box box we'll add "LCD screen cleaner". Leave the No Subtitle box selected.

We will fill in a brief description of the cleaner as seen below.

The next page is the Pricing and duration page where you can specify the starting price, reserve price, buy now price and length of the auction.

In the Start price box we'll enter 1 and leave the reserve price as "Same as the start price". We will leave the Buy Now box empty for now and also leave the "Authenticated members only" box unchecked.

We will also leave the Listing duration as 7 days. This is the default setting.

The next screen is the Payment and shipping page where we specify shipping costs and payments. We will leave the Pick-ups box as "No pick-ups" and select "I'll provide free shipping within New Zealand"

In the Accepted payment methods section we will select NZ bank deposit. You can set up your payment instructions if you wish by providing a bank account number for the buyer to deposit to and select " Automatically send my payment instructions when this auction is won." box. Otherwise you can simply get in touch with the buyer by email when the auction has finished to communicate your bank details. For now, I'll leave this blank.

The next page is the Photos page where you can add photos of your item. You can add up to 20 photos for free. We will click on the "Add photos" button and select a photo of our product from our computer to add. Note the advice about the photo size etc.

The Extras page allows you to select various "extra" packages to promote your product. You can read more about these packages in the picture below. For now, we will click the "or Select Basic" to create a basic (see free) listing.

The last screen before you complete your listing is a conformation page where you can check all the details are correct before you list your product. Once you have checked the detials are correct, click the "Start my Auction" button.

Congratulations! You have listed an item for sale. You can check on your items for sale in the "My Trade Me" menu in the top navigation bar

Gmail.com

A guide to using Making and using a Gmail account.

Gmail is one of the worlds most popular emailing systems. For free they will provide you with an email that's intuitive, efficient, and useful. 15 GB of storage, less spam, and mobile access. This makes it a great everyday email to keep in contact with your friends and family.

Objectives of this tutorial

Learn how to sign up to Gmail

Learn how to choose a secure Username and Password

Learn how to navigate around your account

Learn how to send, recieve and delete emails

  • Updated: October 2018
  • Author: Rob
  • Category: Emails

Creating an Account part 1

The objective for this lesson is create your very own Gmail account

Step 1. Open your Web browser and type in www.google.com

Step 2. Search for gmail in the google search bar and press the enter key

Step 3. Select the top link

Step 4. Select the signup option

The next section will go more indepth about choosing a login

Creating an Account part 2

The objective of the second part of this is to help you choose an appropriate username and secure password that are easily remembered.

The sign-up stage

By this stage you should be looking at the following screen

Step 1. The first step is to fill out your first and last names.

Selecting a Username

Step 2. This is where we will be choosing our personalise email address. You may automatically have a pre filled email address. This is often your name plus a large quantity of numbers, for example john235890@gmail.com. This is a terrible address as the numbers will make it extremely hard to remember. It is a good idea to use some of the following FIRSTNAME.LASTNAME@gmail.com, NICKNAME.LASTNAME@gmail.com. If these are unavailible then adding your year of birth on the end is usually availible. The key thing to remember is that you are able to remember the address easily for later use

Choosing a Password

Step 3. This is where we choose a password. Gmail requires the password to contain a mix of letters, numbers and symbols. The password length must also be over 8 characters long.

Choosing a password that is secure but memnorable is a fine line to walk. Most passwords that involve a letters and numbers are very easy to forget. To avoid this I have a few suggestions that will put you on the right track in regards to choosing something that is memorable as well as practicle. Using a lefe event plus the year that it happened is a good way to get a password that you will remember but is very unlikely to be hacked. Something like a favourite pet and the year you got it, like Woofers2012. You can apply the same rule to your first car or even a vacation you have been on. Its upto you to decide what you want to use but these are a few suggestions.


Once you are happy with your details. Press the next button at the bottom right.

Creating an Account part 3

The objective of the third part of this is to explain what you can use for account security and finish the sign up

The last step

By this stage you should be looking at the following screen


Setting up a recovery option is not essential to creating your account but can be useful incase you forget your log in details. (See the next two steps for details.)

Step 1. (Optional Step) This is where you are given the option to enter your phone number incase you for get your password or username. If we set a recovery option when we go to reset our account Gmail will send a code to the nuber you have listed and allow you to regain access.

Step 2. (Optional Step) This is where you can use another email address to be used in case of recovery. For this you can use a trusted family member if you would like to. (If you would like to use a family member Please remember to ask for their permission first.)

Step 3. Here all we need to do is simply enter our date of birth.

Step 4. Now we get the option to choose our gender if we wish.


Once you are happy with your details. Press the next button at the bottom right.

Final Step. All we need to do now is read the terms and conditions and if they are agreeable click next. This will now load up our new Gmail acoount. Please see the next steps to learn more about your new account.

Introduction to your Gmail account

The objective of this intro is to get familliar with your new account

First Steps

If this is the first time you are logging in, complete setting up your account and turn all the circles to green ticks.

You should see something simillar to the below picture, Although if this is the first time using it you will only have a welcome email from Gmail

Menu

The most import part of your email account is the menu, from here you can control everything from deleting old emails to creating new ones.

Three dashes - Found at the top left corner will allow you to open and close the menu when you click it.

Compose - Pressing this button will open a new email.

Inbox - This is where you will find your Primary, Social and Promotion inboxs. As you can see I have 3 unread messages in my Primary account.

Starred - This will hold any emails that you have starred for future reference.

Snoozed - Any emails that have been snoozed will be in here untill they are scheduled to remind you.

Sent - This is a record of any emails that you have sent.

Drafts - Any emails that have been started but not sent will be automatically saved here until you are able to complete or delete them.

Exploring the 3 main Inboxes

The objective of this stage is to diferentiate between the three main inboxes.

Primary

Your Primary inbox is where you will recieve mail from people. This is the most important of the inboxes and you should try to keep it as neat and tidy to ensure its functional for you.

Social

This email box is reserved for any emails you recieve from social meadia platforms, like Facebook, Twitter or Instagram

Promotions

This inbox will be filled with fliers and advertisements that you will recieve from any busniess that you have subscribed to this might be the Wharehouse or Noel Leaming. You don't have to respond to these and if you wish to unsubscribe from them you often can.

How to send an email

The objective for this page is to learn how to send an email.

Composing

To create a new email all you need to do is click "compose" in the top left hand corner.

This will open the new email pannel in the bottom right hand corner.

Recipient Details

To: - Recipients are the people you are sending the email to. You will need to type the email address for each recipient.

Cc: - Cc stands for carbon copy. This is used when you want to send an email to someone who is not the main recipient. This helps to keep these people in the loop while letting them know that they probably don't need to reply to your message.

Bcc: - Bcc stands for blind carbon copy. It works almost the same way as Cc, except that all of the email address in the Bcc fields are hidden, making it ideal when emailing a large number of recipients or when privacy is needed.

Subject: - The body is the actual text of the email. Generally, you'll write this just like a normal letter, with a greeting, one or more paragraphs, and a closing with your name.

Text Toolbar

Formatting - Click the Formatting button to access formatting options. Formatting allows you to change the look and feel of your message. For example, you can change the font style, size, and color, as well as include links.

Attachments

Attachments - An attachment is a file (like an image or a document) that is sent along with the email message. Gmail allows you to include multiple attachments. Click the attachment button to include an attachment with the email..

This has only been a brief introduction to the capabilities of Gmail. if you still want to know more you can try doing some of these exercises at home. If you want to learn more after you have finished the exercises, a quick google search will provide you with a wealth of information
